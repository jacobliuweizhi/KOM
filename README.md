## ðŸŒ Background

Knee osteoarthritis (KOA) affects more than **600 million people worldwide**, leading to chronic pain, functional decline, and increased healthcare burden. Although clinical guidelines emphasize **personalized** and **multidisciplinary** management, real-world practice often falls short due to:

* Limited clinical resources and time
* High variability in patient presentations
* Lack of standardized assessment workflows
* Existing tools addressing only isolated tasks (e.g., imaging or prediction)

To address these unmet needs, we developed **KOM**, a multi-agent AI system designed to support the **entire clinical workflow** of KOA management.
